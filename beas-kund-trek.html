Overview

Himachal is in the western Himalayas. Covering an area of 55,673 square kilometres it is a mountainous state. Most of the state lies on the foothills of the Dhauladhar Range. At 6,816 m Reo Purgyil is the highest mountain peak in the state of Himachal Pradesh.

One of the most popular treks from Manali, Himachal Pradesh. the trek has its own religious as well as beatific significance. The bliss of the mountains, the tranquillity of the meadows, the pristine air and the sweet hush of waters rushing against the rocks, such is the beauty of the Beas Kund trek.

You start from an altitude of 8,156 ft at Solang Valley and you reach the highest point of 12,772 ft at the lake at Beas Kund. This means you gain around 4,616 ft over just three days of trekking. This can make the trek quite demanding.

As the name suggests, the trek is to the Beas Kund, the trek to the inception of the river Beas. This in itself makes it far more interesting than any other trek, because you get to witness an alpine glacier melting away into a little stream which then takes the form of a river of an enormity as that of Beas.

But it is not the simple view of the glacier and the lake that makes this trek as astounding as it is, but it is the well-trodden path of the trek which takes you through the most spectacular views of the mountains and valleys.
Another fact that adds a feather to this trek is the limited time in which it can be completed.

  • Nearest Town: Manali
  • Duration of the event: 3 Day/2 Night
  • Difficulty Level: Easy to Moderate
  • Transportation: Provided by “Miles to Trek”
  • Food: Veg Food Provided
  • Accommodation: Tent Stay on sharing Basis
  • Height Gain: 4471M

ITINERARY

(Day 1) Manali to Bakarthach via Solang valley – Dundhi
  • After breakfast we proceed to Dundhi by cab and after that trek will start to Bakarthach. The trail leads uphill from Solang Valley through green meadows and trees of alder, pine and fir to the grazing pastures of Bakarthach. Camp here for the night with excellent views of the surrounding mountain peaks. overnight at camp
(Day 2) Bakarthach– BEAS KUND (4471M) & BACK:
  • The trail again climbs steeply towards the beautiful glaciated lake of Beas Kund which is also one of the sources of the River Beas. Get close views of Hanuman Tibba, Friendship and other famous peaks of Manali.
(Day 3) Bakarthach – DHUNDHI – SOLANG:
  • Early in the morning After breakfast we proceed to Solang valley. (Dundhi to Solang valley we proceed by cab) And end of our services
